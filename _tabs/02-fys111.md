## FYS 111 — The AI Experience

> FYS 111 is the hour before COM 216 — one credit of hands-on practice that sets up the four credits of reading and writing that follow it.

If you take both courses, Tuesdays and Thursdays look like this:

| Time | Course | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 9:00 – 10:00 am | **FYS 111** | Hands-on work: prompting, documenting, building tools |
| 10:00 am – 12:00 pm | **COM 216** | Reading, discussion, and writing — using what you just practiced |

The point is that you're never handed a tool you haven't already used.

### What you do in FYS 111

**Build tools for college.** You'll construct working systems for managing your syllabi, your time, and your coursework — using AI to build them, and then actually using them. The tools work for these two courses and travel with you into everything after.

**Learn to work with a model, not just ask it questions.** The goal is to put the model into the loop of your thinking — starting tasks by asking how AI can be part of them, pushing back when it goes wrong, redirecting when it drifts. The judgment stays yours. The model is there to think with, draft what you'll revise, bring more than you could have gathered alone. You keep a transcript of every working conversation — that record matters, because in COM 216, the transcript is the work.

**Understand where it goes wrong.** People talk to AI like a someone. They feel met by it — and that isn't simply a mistake, because the thing genuinely answers. A not-person we relate to as a someone: we've never had that before, and we don't yet have good instincts for it. FYS 111 looks holistically at what happens when humans develop relationships with these entities — not to frighten you or freak you out, but because responsible use requires knowing the risks, not just the capabilities.

### Why it's split into two courses

Splitting them keeps the hands-on hour focused on practice and the two-hour class focused on ideas — without either one crowding the other. What you work through at 9am shows up in your reading at 10; what trips you up in COM 216 is something to bring back to FYS 111.

By the end of the semester you'll have five credits and a documented record of how your thinking with AI actually changed.
