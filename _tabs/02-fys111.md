## FYS 111 — The AI Experience

> FYS 111 is the hour before COM 216 — one credit of hands-on practice that sets up the four credits of reading and writing that follow it.

If you're in the bundle, Tuesdays and Thursdays look like this, and will frequently involve small-group or independent work. It is critical that you plan to attend class at this time throughout the semester:

| Time | Course | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 9:00 – 10:00 am | **FYS 111** | Hands-on practice: prompting, documenting, comparing tools |
| 10:00 am – 12:00 pm | **COM 216** | Reading, discussion, and writing — using what you just practiced |

The point of FYS 111 is that you're never handed a tool you haven't already used. By the time COM 216 asks you to read a difficult text with AI, you've already practiced prompting, already seen what a documented conversation looks like, and already have a paid account open and working — not a free-tier version you're rationing.

### What you actually do in FYS 111

**Earn the AI Literacy Learner's Permit.**
Five hours of structured, supervised practice — reading, thinking, and writing with a model while someone who knows what they're doing watches and gives feedback. You leave with a stack of transcripts that prove you can do this, and with permission to use AI independently in any SUNY Poly course that requires it.

**Start toward a Commercial License.**
Once you're comfortable having a conversation with a model, you start working with AI that acts on its own — tools that complete multi-step tasks without you prompting every single step. This is the next level up from "I asked it a question and it answered."

**Work toward a Google Career Certificate.**
The skills you're practicing map directly onto certificates employers actually recognize. Completing them runs alongside your coursework, not as an extra thing bolted on top.

**Use real, paid tools from day one.**
Everyone in the cohort gets a working account on a professional-tier model, set up before classes start. No waitlists, no free-tier caps, no scrambling to find your own way in.

---

### Why it's split into two courses

FYS 111 and COM 216 could be one long class, but splitting them means the skills hour stays focused on building muscle memory, and the thinking hour stays focused on ideas — without either one getting rushed to make room for the other. What you practice at 9am shows up in your reading and writing at 10am; what trips you up in COM 216 becomes something to work through back in FYS 111.

By the end of the semester you'll have five credits, the Learner's Permit, a start on the Commercial License and a Career Certificate, and — more usefully than any of those on their own — your own transcripts showing exactly how your thinking with AI changed over fifteen weeks.
