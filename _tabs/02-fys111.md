## FYS 111 — The AI Experience

> FYS 111 is the skills companion to COM 216. One credit. Same days. The hour before. For students enrolled in both, Tuesday and Thursday run from 9 to noon — skills first, framework second.

### Schedule

| Time | Course | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 9:00 – 10:00 am | **FYS 111** | The AI Experience — 1 credit. Skills, credentials, practice. |
| 10:00 am – 12:00 pm | **COM 216** | AI and the Human Future — 4 credits. History, framework, reading and writing. |

FYS 111 builds the practical infrastructure for everything COM 216 asks you to do. You will not be asked to use AI tools you don't know how to operate. You will not be limited to free-tier access. You will not be expected to figure out protocol on your own.

### What FYS 111 covers

**Credential — AI Literacy Learner's Permit**
Supervised compositional AI use: reading, thinking, and writing with large language models. Five hours of structured practice producing transcripts and artifacts. Required before independent AI use in any course.

**Advanced Credential — Commercial License**
Agentic AI operation: systems that act, not just generate. Working with autonomous tools, multi-step pipelines, and directive AI in applied contexts. For students ready to move beyond conversation into operation.

**Professional Certification — Google Career Certificates**
Industry-recognized credentials integrated into the course sequence. Applied AI skills mapped to professional workflows. Certificate completion tracked alongside the LP and Commercial License.

**Access — Model Access, All Students**
Every student in the cohort receives access to professional-tier large language models. Not free-tier limitations. Not bring-your-own-account. Full access, same tools, from day one.

---

### The bundle

For cohort students, FYS 111 and COM 216 are not two separate courses taking up schedule space. They are one integrated experience: the skills hour and the thinking hour, back to back, with the same people. What you practice in FYS 111 at 9am you apply in COM 216 at 10am. What you encounter intellectually in COM 216 you bring back to FYS 111 to test, extend, and document.

The LP, the transcripts, the first contact protocol — all of it runs across both courses simultaneously. By the end of the semester, you have five credits, two credentials, professional certifications, and a documented record of how you think with AI. That record is yours to carry into every course that follows.
