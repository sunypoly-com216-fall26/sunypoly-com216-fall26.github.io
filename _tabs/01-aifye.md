## AI in the First Year Experience

> AI-FYE is a two-course bundle for incoming students who want to learn how to live and work with AI from day one — not as an afterthought, but as the foundation of how they read, think, and write in college.

Two courses. Five credits. Two paid AI models. A professional credential. All of it running at once, in the same time block, with the same cohort, from the first week of the semester.

### The bundle at a glance

**FYS 111 — The AI Experience**
*1 credit · T/TH 9:00–10:00 am*

Skills, credentials, and tools. The Learner's Permit. The Commercial License pathway. Google Career Certificates. You leave with documented proof of what you know how to do.

**COM 216 — AI and the Human Future**
*4 credits · T/TH 10:00 am–12:00 pm*

The history and intellectual framework. From the oral world to the transformer era. Why this moment is different, what it asks of you, and how to think clearly inside it.

**Two Paid Models — Professional-Tier AI, From Day One**
*Model access included for all cohort students*

Every student in the cohort receives a semester account — **Claude or ChatGPT** (to be confirmed) — and access to [Google AI Pro for Students](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/products/gemini/google-ai-pro-students-learning/) (Gemini). Not free-tier limitations. Not bring-your-own-account. Two professional models, covered, from orientation day.

---

### How the bundle works

FYS 111 and COM 216 share the same cohort and run back to back on Tuesday and Thursday. The skills hour at 9am feeds directly into the intellectual work at 10am. What you practice in FYS 111 — prompting, transcript documentation, model comparison — is what you use in COM 216 to read historical texts, think through arguments, and write.

The LP orientation happens before the semester begins. All students and all faculty complete it together. By the first day of class, the cohort has a shared working environment, a shared protocol, and a shared record of what first contact looked like.

This is not a course about AI. It is a first-year experience organized around the idea that AI literacy is a practice — one you build by doing it, documenting it, and examining what you did. The bundle gives you the tools, the framework, and the time to do that properly.
